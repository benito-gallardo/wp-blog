It’s natural to have questions about ecommerce web design.

What do you want your site to look like? How do you know what you want? Where should you get started?

Join me as two of our professional design experts, Shelley and Jessica, walk me through several questions about web design basics that we receive from customers when they begin on their site.


 

Here’s the transcript if you’d like to read along:

Matt: Hi, everybody! Welcome to this week’s edition of Ecommerce Round Table. My Name’s Matt. I’m excited to be joined by Shelly and Jessica, two of our fabulous design experts here at Volusion. Shelly works with people right before they get into the design process and answers a lot of questions, while Jessica is on the creative end and helps build the design of your dreams.

If you’ve been keeping up with us over the past couple of weeks, we’ve talked about the importance of design and branding and some specific elements that you can design and modify on your site to make it look great. So, with this in mind, we’d like to really help out, dispel some myths and answer questions that you might have about web design with our FAQs.

The first one we get quite often, and I can definitely relate to, is, “I’m not creative. I know I want something pretty, but I don’t know what I want. How can you guys help me?”

 

Shelly: That’s probably what I hear the most from the pre-sales call. People will call in and say, “I want something nice. I want something pretty. I want something that looks like Nike.com” or whoever it might be and that’s perfectly fine. That’s a great place to start. I’ll work with you and get any ideas that you might have, making sure to find a package that’s going to fit your needs and your budget. Once we get that taken care of, we get you handed over to the design team and they’re going to be with you from that point forward.

 

Jessica: Yeah, I definitely agree 100%. You do not need to be creative in order to go through this process. We have a lot of people who are there to help you. As soon as the design team gets your project, you’ll be contacted by project coordinators and they’ll help guide you from start to finish. We’ll get you a design questionnaire where you’ll describe what your business does, what you need on the site and some sites you like the look and feel of. This information is vital to getting you the site that you really want.

 

Matt: Nice! So if you have an idea of what you like, then we’ll make it into something for you, no problem. Cool! Secondly, there are a lot of buzz words going around in ecommerce and online marketing, and one of the biggest ones “blog.” With that in mind, can I have a blog on my site? Can I design it? Why should I have one?

 

Shelly: We don’t necessarily have blog software built into the Volusion platform, but you most certainly can have a blog. We can integrate with pretty much any blog company out there. The one we work with the most is probably Blogger.

Setting up a blog is a great thing to do. It’s really easy to connect it to your store, and our tech support is always there to help. From the design side of things, we do actually have a blogger design service that we offer if you want to connect the design of your Volusion site with the design of your blog.

 

Jessica:  Yeah, definitely. What you would do is place a link to your blog on your ecommerce site. If you’re using blogger, we have a blogger template design service that’ll help you carry your branding over from your site to your blog.  We would take your colors and your logo and put them there, as well as add important links back to your ecommerce site. We would also add a major call-out so that customers can easily go back to shopping because that’s the number one reason they’re there.

 

Matt:  Also, as a side note, if you’re already thinking about having a blog even before you’ve designed your ecommerce site, you’re way ahead of the game and going to be doing yourself a service by launching them in tandem. So, along the lines of buzz words and topics that we hear frequently about, another one is landing pages. Whether you’re trying to optimize one or create one, we really can’t start until we answer the question, “What is a landing page?”

 

Jessica:  There are a lot of definitions for a landing page, so it’s probably easiest to give an example. Say you send out an email blast to let your customers know, “Hey! This year we have 50% off our men’s line,” and in that email, you link them to a specific page that explains more about that sale and then leads them to those specific products. That page you sent your customers to is a landing page. You could also use a landing page to differentiate between retail versus wholesale or, you can use it as a splash page (an entrance page to your site).

 

Matt: So, it seems like a landing page is something that you can use to introduce people to your business and your website without sending them to the homepage?

 

Jessica: Yeah, absolutely. It’s a different, ideally more specific page to land on.

 

Matt: Well, that explains it. Alright, cool. And like we touched on the different elements and pieces of your overall site design puzzle, this question one definitely took me a while to get, so don’t feel bad if you don’t know. What is the difference between your template and your homepage?

 

Shelly:  That’s a really good question. When you’re talking design with various shops, people tend to throw these words around to mean slightly different things. Within Volusion, when we say “template,” we mean the background, the header, the navigation and the footer. In other words, the framework that’s going to repeat on every single page of your site. The homepage, on the other hand, is specifically just that first introductory page.

 

Jessica:  Yeah, I think that’s a really great description of it. Another way of looking at it is that the homepage is the center area on the first page of your site, between the header and the footer.

 

Matt: Just to get things straight, the template is essentially the skeleton of the site that you’ll see on every single page that you go to. The homepage is that center content that gives customers an introduction to your business and an idea of what you have to offer.

 

Shelly:  Exactly.

 

Jessica:  Yep.

 

Matt: Okay, cool. Last but not least, you go to different sites and you see a lot of different treatments of design, branding and the whole nine yards. Some of these sites include video, flash, music and all this other fancy-shmancy stuff. So, can we have it all? Are there any limitations? And what are your recommendations on adding these extra features or capabilities?

 

Jessica:  I don’t know that there are any limitations for the flash or the music or things like that. It’s basically just going to come down to what we recommend. As far as music, you can definitely have it on your site. I don’t know that I recommend it, though, because it can distract your customers. Instead of concentrating on what you sell, your customers will be searching for the “Mute” or “Off” switch on the music. So, I definitely don’t think that I’d recommend that.

For flash, it really depends on what Flash is being used for. If it’s for a slideshow, I don’t recommend using it because it’s not good for SEO, and you have other, SEO-friendly options like a Javascript slideshow. (Plus, Javascript slideshows are easier to update as well.) So, again, there are technically no limitations; it’s just that certain things tend to be better for your store.

 

Matt: Gotcha. Well, personal preferences dictate everything, but if you have a chance to get some insights from the experts, definitely heed their advice. We’ll just leave it at that. Shelly, Jessica, thank you guys so much for joining us today and thank you as always for joining us. If you have any questions about ecommerce, selling online, ecommerce designs specifically, feel free to let us know. We’re always happy to help. And from us to you, happy selling.
